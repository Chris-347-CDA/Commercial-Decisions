Ref. 00 — The problem

Businesses rarely struggle because they lack information — most have more data than ever. They struggle because decisions get harder as the business grows: more opinions in the room, more at stake, less time to pressure-test the thinking before committing to it.

Getting an answer has never been easier. Knowing whether it's the right answer — and what to do about it — hasn't gotten any easier at all. That's not a data problem. It's a decision-making problem, and it's exactly where I help.

Ref. 01 — Decisions I help with

If you're asking questions like these, we should probably talk.

Growth

Should we expand?

Can we afford another location? Is now the right time — and what happens if we grow faster than the business can cope with?

Investment

Should we invest?

What return are we actually expecting, what assumptions is that built on, and what happens if we're wrong?

Performance

Why are results changing?

Is it volume, price or cost? What's controllable, and where should we focus first?

People

Should we recruit?

Or do we need better systems, clearer accountability, or a different structure entirely?

Strategy

Where do we go next?

Which opportunity creates the greatest value — and what should we stop doing to make room for it?

Confidence

We've picked a path. Are we right?

Sometimes the decision is already made in your head. You just want someone experienced to pressure-test it before you say yes out loud.

Ref. 03 — The Decision Framework

Every engagement follows the same structured thinking.

Not a template to fill in — a discipline for making sure the business is solving the right problem, not just the loudest one.

01

Define

What decision are we actually trying to make? Everything starts with getting this precisely right.

02

Understand

Separate fact from assumption. Understand what we know — and what we still need to find out.

03

Challenge

Pressure-test the thinking. Challenge assumptions and bring in perspectives the room might be missing.

04

Explore

Evaluate the realistic options, understand the trade-offs, and strip out unnecessary complexity.

05

Decide

Choose the path that gives the greatest confidence — not simply the loudest opinion in the room.

06

Learn

Capture what the decision taught us. Every important decision should make the next one easier.

Confidence comes from understanding — not guesswork.

Ref. 04 — Why work with me

I've spent my career in strategic planning for a large global business — covering insights, planning and forecasting for decisions with real commercial consequences. Most of that has been inside large organisations, but I work just as comfortably with smaller, founder-led businesses and startups making their first big commercial calls. The scale changes; the thinking doesn't.

What I bring isn't just the analysis. It's the ability to take complex data and competing scenarios and turn them into a decision your team can actually act on — delivered with a genuinely human approach, not jargon or theatre.

I'm not here to make decisions for you. I'm here to ask better questions, challenge assumptions, and help you reach decisions you can confidently stand behind — so your business makes its next decision better than the last.
